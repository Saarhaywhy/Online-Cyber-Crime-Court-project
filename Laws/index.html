What is Cyber Law?

Cyber law, sometimes called "internet law," is the branch of law that governs how individuals utilize the internet. Both criminal and civil laws apply to the use of computers and the internet. Cyber law refers to any legislation or rule that affects how individuals utilize computers, cellphones, the internet, and other related technologies.


Another name for cyber law is digital law. Laws governing electronic communication vary along with technology. All of the ways that individuals connect and communicate using contemporary technology are covered under cyber law.

NIGERIA DATA PROTECTION REGULATION

The NDPR is the pioneer, comprehensive Regulation on data protection in Nigeria. NITDA, through a stakeholder model has developed this NDPR Implementation Framework (the ‘Framework’) as a guide to assist data controllers and data administrators/processors understand the controls and measures they need to introduce into their operations in order to comply with the NDPR.

Computer Fraud and Abuse Act

The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) makes it illegal to knowingly access a computer without permission, among other things. Congress initially passed the CFAA, which is codified at 18 U.S.C. 1030, in 1986 to combat different types of "computer crime." This was usually taken to mean "hacking or trespassing into computer systems or data" at the time. The law has subsequently undergone several expansions and modifications as legislators attempt to stay up with innovation and technological improvements.

CyberCrime Prohibition Prevention Act 2015

The  Act  provides  an  effective,  unified  and  comprehensive  legal,  regulatory  and  institutional framework  for  the  prohibition,  prevention,  detection,  prosecution  and  punishment  of cybercrimes  in  Nigeria.  This  act  also  ensures  the  protection  of  critical  national  information infrastructure,  and  promotes  cybersecurity  and  the  protection  of  computer  systems  and networks,  electronic  communications,  data  and  computer  programs,  intellectual  property and  privacy  rights.
